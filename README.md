# ⚡ Solid State Tesla Coil (Slayer Exciter)

A **Solid State Tesla Coil (SSTC)** built using the **Slayer Exciter circuit** to demonstrate the principles of **high-voltage generation**, **electromagnetic induction**, **resonance**, and **electromagnetic interference (EMI)**. The project is intended for educational and laboratory purposes to study electromagnetic compatibility (EMC), resonance, and wireless energy transfer concepts.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project uses a **BD139 transistor-based Slayer Exciter circuit** powered by a **12V DC supply** to generate high-frequency, high-voltage oscillations through a resonant transformer.

The Tesla Coil demonstrates:

- High-voltage generation
- Resonant energy transfer
- Electromagnetic induction
- Electromagnetic Interference (EMI)
- Basics of Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC)

The project is useful for understanding how resonant transformers operate and how electromagnetic interference affects nearby electronic systems in laboratory environments.

# ⚙ Working Principle

The project is based on the **Slayer Exciter** topology.

### Step 1 – Initial Bias

The 12V supply provides current through the **2.2 kΩ resistor** to the base of the BD139 transistor, switching it ON.

### Step 2 – Primary Coil Excitation

Current flows through the primary winding, producing a rapidly changing magnetic field.

### Step 3 – Electromagnetic Induction

The changing magnetic field induces a voltage in the secondary winding.

### Step 4 – Feedback Oscillation

The induced voltage drives the transistor into continuous switching, creating self-sustaining oscillations at the resonant frequency.

### Step 5 – High Voltage Generation

The secondary winding steps up the voltage significantly, producing high-frequency, high-voltage output at the aluminium foil top load.

# 🚀 How to Build

1. Wind the secondary coil (800–850 turns of 30 AWG wire).
2. Wind the primary coil (3–5 turns).
3. Assemble the Slayer Exciter circuit.
4. Attach a heat sink to the BD139 transistor.
5. Connect the aluminium foil top load.
6. Power the circuit using a regulated **12V DC 5A** adapter.
7. Observe the high-frequency operation and resonant behavior.

---

# ⚠ Safety Precautions

- High voltages are generated during operation.
- Avoid touching the secondary coil or top load while powered.
- Keep electronic devices away to avoid unintended electromagnetic interference.
- Operate only in a controlled laboratory or educational environment.
- Use appropriate insulation and protective equipment.
